Winston Churchill

1874 - 1965

"We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields and in the streets, we shall fight in the hills; we shall never surrender, and even if, which I do not for a moment believe, this island or a large part of it were subjugated and starving, then our Empire beyond the seas, armed and guarded by the British Fleet, would carry on the struggle, until, in Gods good time, the New World, with all its power and might, steps forth to the rescue and the liberation of the old."

Timeline

Early Life

  • 1874

    - Born in Blenheim Palace in Oxfordshire to Lord Randolph and Lady Jennie Churchill.

  • 1895

    - Commissioned as a second lieutenant in the 4th Queen's Own Hussars regiment of the British Army, based at Aldershot.

  • 1900

    - Stood as one of the Conservative candidates at Oldham in the October 1900 general election, securing a narrow victory to become a Member of Parliament at age 25.

Asquith government

  • 1908

    - Appointed President of the Board of Trade at the age of 33 to become the youngest Cabinet member since 1866.

  • 1910

    - Promoted to Home Secretary, giving him control over the police and prison services where he implemented a prison reform programme. Measures included a distinction between criminal and political prisoners, with prison rules for the latter being relaxed.

  • 1911

    - Asquith appointed Churchill First Lord of the Admiralty and he took up official residence at Admiralty House. Over the next two and a half years he focused on naval preparation, visiting naval stations and dockyards, seeking to improve morale, and scrutinising German naval developments.

World War II

  • 1939

    - Appointed on 3 September 1939, the day Britain declared war on Germany, as First Lord of the Admiralty and he joined Chamberlain's war cabinet.

  • 1940

    - Addressed the commons, at the height of the Battle of Britain, to outline the war situation. In the middle of this speech, he made a statement that created a famous nickname for the RAF fighter pilots involved in the battle:

  • 1941

    - Declared war on Japan after the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor followed by their invasion of Malaya and, on the 8th. Three days later came the joint declaration of war by Germany and Italy against the United States.

  • 1943

    - Met Roosevelt at the Casablanca Conference, which lasted ten days. It was also attended by General Charles de Gaulle on behalf of the Free French Forces. Stalin had hoped to attend but declined because of the situation at Stalingrad

  • 1945

    - Accepted Germany's surrender at the SHAEF headquarters in Reims the Allies . The next day was Victory in Europe Day (VE Day) when Churchill broadcast to the nation that Germany had surrendered and that a final ceasefire on all fronts in Europe would come into effect at one minute past midnight that night.

2nd Premiership

  • 1951

    - Became the Prime Minister again when the Conservatives won the general election in October 1951 with an overall majority of 17 seats, remaining in office until his resignation on 5 April 1955.

  • 1952

    - He made four official transatlantic visits from January 1952 to July 1954. Churchill feared a global conflagration and firmly believed that the only way to preserve peace and freedom was to build on a solid foundation of friendship and co-operation between Britain and America.

  • 1953

    - Suffered a serious stroke and became partially paralyzed down one side on the evening of 23 June 1953.

Death and Memorials

  • 1965

    - He suffered his final stroke on 12 January 1965. He died nearly two weeks later on the 24th, which was the seventieth anniversary of his father's death.

  • 1973

    - His statue in Parliament Square was unveiled by his widow Clementine and is one of only twelve in the square, all of prominent political figures, including Churchill's friend Lloyd George and his India policy nemesis Gandhi.

  • 1999

    - The United States Navy honoured him in 1999 by naming a new Arleigh Burke-class destroyer as the USS Winston S. Churchill.
